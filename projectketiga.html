WHAT I LEARNED DURING THE ACADEMY:

    • Root Cause: Root cause analysis involves identifying the underlying reason or source of a problem or issue. It helps in understanding the fundamental cause to effectively address and solve the problem at its core.
    • User Flow: User flow refers to the path or sequence of steps that a user takes to navigate through a website, application, or system. It outlines the actions and interactions a user goes through to accomplish a specific goal or task.
    • User Journey: User journey describes the entire experience of a user as they interact with a product, service, or platform. It encompasses all touchpoints and interactions, both online and offline, and helps understand the user's emotions, motivations, and pain points throughout their experience.
    • Empathy Map: An empathy map is a visual tool used to gain a deeper understanding of users by capturing their thoughts, feelings, behaviors, and needs. It helps in developing empathy for users, improving user-centered design, and guiding decision-making based on user insights.
    • UI Kit: A UI (User Interface) kit is a collection of pre-designed elements, such as buttons, icons, forms, and other interface components, that maintain consistency and provide a starting point for designing user interfaces. UI kits save time and effort by offering reusable design assets.
    • User Persona: A user persona is a fictional representation of a target user group. It captures demographic information, behaviors, motivations, goals, and pain points of a specific user segment. Personas help in understanding and designing for the needs and preferences of different user types.
    • Synthesize: Synthesizing refers to the process of combining and analyzing information, data, or research findings to identify patterns, insights, and key takeaways. It involves distilling large amounts of information into meaningful and actionable insights.
    • Low Fidelity Design: Low fidelity design refers to rough or basic representations of a design concept or interface. It focuses on the core functionality and structure without much visual detail. Low fidelity designs are often used in the early stages of prototyping to quickly explore and iterate ideas.
    • High Fidelity Design: High fidelity design refers to a more detailed and refined representation of a design concept or interface. It incorporates visual elements, typography, colors, and interactive components to closely resemble the final product. High fidelity designs are used for testing and communicating the final design vision.
    • Impact-Effort Diagram: An impact-effort diagram, also known as a prioritization matrix, is a visual tool used to evaluate and prioritize tasks, features, or ideas based on their potential impact and the effort required to implement them. It helps in making informed decisions by considering the balance between impact and effort.
